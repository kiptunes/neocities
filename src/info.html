why I maintain this site

I have always loved old internet aesthetics and will forever be a proponent of learning to code, especially in today's age when corporate companies are enforcing a narrative that knowing the ins and outs of your own technology is some sort of inaccessible high-skill feat. It's a shame it has to be said, but, as access to information continues to become more and more digital, basic tech literacy should be a staple of learning to be on the internet at all.

That being said, you don't have to know how to publish an app, or code a game, or make a website, although those are all fun and viable things to do if they interest you. But, please, know what your directories are for on your computer, know what companies are doing with your private information, and know to recognize malicious links.

As someone visiting a neocities page, this is all probably old news to you, but it's an important part of the reason why I created this site, so I felt obligated to put this spiel here. I know my friends have heard me say all this a hundred times. At the risk of sounding overly earnest, I just believe in the return of autonomy to the user that a personal page offers. Customization is a sort of freedom, and every hand-coded website is an expression of self, to me. Bad websites are beautiful and I'll die on this hill.

If you do happen to be new to coding, and you're looking for a starting point, hi! Its versatility may feel overwhelming when you are just beginning. Please don't let it daunt you! Making a website is a wonderful way to introduce yourself to programming. I hope you have fun if you choose to make one, too.

And thank you for visiting my silly corner of the internet! :)

About the Site

I made this site to use as a css without js sandbox ! It uses the Bulma CSS framework (specifically the alt version without helpers) for theming, responsiveness, and other components like the breadcrumbs at the bottom of this page.

FAQ

How long did this take you?

According to Wakatime, as of now, 15 hours!

Why not use javascript?

The point of this site at the time of conception was to re-familiarize myself with css, so I wanted to do some silly stuff with css only. In the future, this site will probably use more js! Just not right now.
(Although, others might prefer not to use javascript because it can bloat a website. At the end of the day, it depends on your usecase and who you want your site to be seen by.)

Why use a framework?

I wanted to make this site responsive (able to adapt to different screen sizes), and it's just a little bit faster to use a framework to accomplish that. tl;dr I'm just lazy

Can I copy or modify this site's code?

Yes! My code is free use without attribution, but if you also keep the part of the code that uses Bulma CSS, keep in mind that it uses the MIT license, which has different requirements.
